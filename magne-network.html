Two-Layer Chain Architecture

MAGNE L1 handles ownership logic. MHash L2 handles scalable execution. Together they form a complete stack from device to chain.

L1 — MAGNE

Ownership Layer

MAGNE L1 is the foundational layer designed for secure ownership, wallet logic, and asset-related operations. It is where private keys live, where signing happens, and where the rules of digital asset ownership are enforced.

  • Private key storage & key isolation
  • Transaction signing (hardware-backed)
  • Wallet logic & address management
  • Asset ownership records
  • Staking & validation participation
⚡
Native
L2 — MHASH

Execution Layer

MHash L2 is the scalable execution layer designed for broader applications and lower-friction user interaction. It is where smart contracts run, where dApps live, and where users interact with the broader ecosystem at lower cost.

  • Smart contract execution
  • dApp deployment & hosting
  • Cross-chain bridge operations
  • Low-fee token transfers & swaps
  • Ecosystem service logic

Why the Device as Gateway?

MAGNE is not a phone with a blockchain app added. It is a native gateway — where the device and the chain are designed together from the start.

Keys never leave the device

Private keys are generated and stored in the secure element. Signing happens in hardware. The seed phrase never exists in software. This is not a software wallet with extra steps — it is hardware-grade ownership built into the device architecture.

Chain interaction is native, not app-based

When you access DeFi, a dApp, or a bridge from MAGNE, the interaction flows through the secure element and the MAGNE/MHash stack. You are not trusting a browser extension or a third-party app — you are interacting with the chain through device-native architecture.

One device, multiple chains

The dual-chain architecture means MAGNE L1 handles ownership while MHash L2 handles execution. Users do not need a separate computer or hardware wallet for each chain — the device manages both.

Recovery without cloud

NFC-based encrypted backup means you can recover your assets offline, without a seed phrase written down, without cloud backup, and without trusting a third party. The device is the key and the backup.
